About the Workshop

The 5th Workshop on Computational Topology (ComPer 2025) will take place October 10-14 in hybrid mode at University at Albany, SUNY in New York. This workshop provides a forum to exchange ideas on computational aspects of topological persistence, TDA, and related topics.

The schedule will consist of invited, contributed, and lightining talks in the mornings, and discussions and a poster session for the local participants in the afternoons. Contributed talks and lighting talks with a poster can be suggested in the form of an abstract of at most two pages. A scientific committee will review the submissions and make a selection.

We encourage in-person participation, but equally welcome submissions of researchers that attend remotely.

Submission Details (for contributed talks and lighting talks with poster)

To allow more participants to share their results, this year we have a two-tier acceptance system: contributed talks and lightning talks with posters. The scientific committee will decide whether a submission should be accepted as a contributed talk, or as a lightning talk with poster, or rejected. If you wish to be considered only for a lightning talk with a poster, please state so in your pdf file.
